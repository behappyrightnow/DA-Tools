Uninformed

Select this when you have zero intuition about the experiment or the control. Not a good choice if you believe things like:

Symmetric


Normal Distribution (beta parameterized)
Select this when you have a very strong belief in the following:
  • There is total symmetry in what you think you will see in the experiment
  • The chance of seeing outliers is ridiculously small
In general, this is not a suitable distribution for most situations unless there are strict physical limits in the data or the quantity you are assessing is the sum of other quantities and there are enough samples to make the central limit theorem applicable.

Asymmetric


Skewed Beta Distribution
This is the best scientific choice for most situations because you tend to have some intuition about the situation.
